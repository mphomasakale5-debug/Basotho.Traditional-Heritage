DANCES

  • Mokhibo

    • A rhythmic women dance often performed at celebrations accompanied ny hand clapping and singing.

  • Mamokhorotlo

    • A ceremonial dance performed by men wearing traditional blanketsand hats

  • Mohobelo

    • This is an energetic dance particularly characterized by its high leaps and powerful rhythmic stompoing

  • Litolobonya

    • Performed by young women, a dance and song genre that serves as a rite of passage.

  • Mokorotlo

    • Performed by men originally used by men to prepare for war

  • Maqekha

    • A specific dance that is part of the rites of passage for girls during their initiation into womanhood

  • Motonoso

    • Performed by girls at ceremonies accoumpanied by singing.

  • Letsema

    • A communal work dance where people work together while singing and dancing

  • Liphotha

    • It involves rhythmic movements and chanting to make the return of initiates.

  • Selialia

    • Perfomed by both men and women for the choosing of the groom.

  • Mohlabani

    • A warrior dance mimicking fighting movements often with sticks or spears.
